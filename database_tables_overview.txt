operational flow: from foundational/reference tables → user/identity tables →

===============================================================================
OPERATIONAL FLOW SUMMARY
===============================================================================

1. FIRST: System infrastructure is initialized (cache, jobs, settings,
   academic_years, generations, roles, permissions).

2. SECOND: Users are created and authenticated (users, sessions,
   personal_access_tokens). Profiles are attached (user_profiles,
   professor_profiles, student_profiles).

3. THIRD: Academic structure is built top-down:
   faculties -> departments -> programs -> courses.
   Pivot tables link courses to programs.

4. FOURTH: Course offerings are created for specific semesters, linking
   courses to programs and assigning lecturers. Rooms and schedules
   are defined.

5. FIFTH: Students enroll in programs (student_program_enrollments) and
   then into specific course offerings (student_course_enrollments).

6. SIXTH: Grading categories are defined for courses. Assessments
   (assignments, exams, quizzes) are created within course offerings.

7. SEVENTH: Students submit assignments, take exams/quizzes. Results
   are recorded in exam_results and submissions.

8. EIGHTH: Attendance is tracked daily via QR tokens or manual marking.
   Professor location is verified. Scores auto-calculate (15% weight).

9. NINTH: Communication flows through notifications, announcements,
   announcement_reads, and chat_messages.

10. TENTH: All actions are logged in audit_logs for accountability.
